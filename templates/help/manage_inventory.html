Keep your inventory up-to-date to avoid overselling and disappointed customers. Learn how to manage your product stock effectively on FarmHub.

Why Inventory Management Matters

  • Customer satisfaction: Avoid accepting orders you can't fulfill
  • Accurate listings: Show real-time availability
  • Efficient operations: Know when to harvest or restock
  • Build trust: Reliable stock info builds customer confidence

Viewing Your Inventory

Step 1: Log in to your seller account.
Step 2: Navigate to "Products" in the sidebar.
Step 3: View all your products with their current stock levels.

Updating Stock Levels

Step 1: Find the product you want to update in your Products list.
Step 2: Click "Edit" on the product.
Step 3: Update the "Available" or "Stock" field with the new quantity.
Step 4: Click "Save" to apply the changes.
Update your stock immediately after harvesting new products or when existing stock is sold through other channels.

Best Practices for Inventory Management

Daily Habits

  • Check stock levels each morning
  • Update after each harvest
  • Adjust after processing orders

Planning Ahead

  • Know your harvest schedules
  • Anticipate seasonal demand
  • Plan inventory for peak times (holidays, weekends)

Accurate Counts

  • Physically count stock periodically
  • Reconcile system numbers with actual inventory
  • Account for spoilage or waste

Handling Out-of-Stock Situations

When a product runs out:

  1. Don't panic: It happens to every farmer
  2. Update immediately: Set stock to 0 so no new orders come in
  3. Communicate: If you have pending orders, contact affected customers
  4. Plan: Know when you'll have more available
  5. Restock: Update as soon as new product is ready

Automatic Stock Updates

FarmHub automatically updates your stock when:

  • An order is placed (stock decreases)
  • An order is cancelled (stock may be restored)
Always keep your stock accurate. Overselling leads to cancelled orders and unhappy customers, which affects your shop's reputation.

Seasonal Inventory Tips

Peak Season

  • Increase stock levels to meet demand
  • Update listings more frequently
  • Consider multiple harvests per day

Off Season

  • Set appropriate stock levels
  • Consider removing out-of-season items
  • Focus on products that are available year-round
